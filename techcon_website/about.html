History

TechCon has evolved from a regional event for the utility industry to a broader technology conference. It originated in 1996 as TechCon North America, focused on the utility industry and regional facility tours. Over time, it expanded its reach, attracting a diverse group of attendees and exhibitors. In 2009, Arm TechCon emerged from Arm DevCon, marking a significant shift towards a broader technology focus, including embedded design, automotive, and AI. This evolution reflects the broader trends of technological advancement and the convergence of various industries.
Here's a more detailed look at the evolution: Early Years (1996-2008): TechCon North America started as a regional event in New Orleans, primarily serving the utility industry. It featured facility tours and focused on technical topics relevant to utility companies. The Shift (2009 onwards): Arm DevCon rebranded to Arm TechCon, signifying a move towards a more diverse range of technology topics. This period coincided with the rise of mobile technology and the smartphone revolution. Arm TechCon's Focus: Arm TechCon became a platform for showcasing advancements in areas like embedded design, automotive, high-performance computing, AI, infrastructure, and mobile technology. Independent Organization: Radio TechCon, while sharing the "TechCon" name, is a separate event focused on the UK radio and audio industry, run by TBC Media Ltd. It originated as part of the UK Radio Festival and later became independently organized. Broadening Scope: The evolution of TechCon, whether Arm TechCon or Radio TechCon, reflects the broader trend of technological convergence and the increasing interconnectedness of various industries. Global Reach: Both events, in their respective domains, have seen increasing global participation and influence. Continued Relevance: TechCon events continue to be relevant platforms for showcasing innovation, sharing knowledge, and fostering collaboration within their respective industries.

Mission

TechCon's goals revolve around creating a global forum for advancing thin film and surface engineering, with a mission to educate, network, and drive innovation in industrial vacuum coating and related technologies.

  • Inspire Innovation: Provide a platform where tech enthusiasts, professionals, and startups can showcase and discuss breakthrough ideas and cutting-edge technologies.
  • Promote Learning: Facilitate workshops, keynote speeches, and panel sessions to upskill participants in emerging tech fields like AI, Web3, Cloud Computing, and more.
  • Foster Collaboration: Connect individuals and organizations across industries to encourage partnerships, networking, and collaborative projects.
  • Empower Diversity: Create an inclusive environment where voices from all backgrounds, especially underrepresented communities in tech, are amplified and valued.
  • Drive Real-World Impact: Ensure discussions and showcases are not just theoretical but applicable, pushing participants towards solutions that can address global and local challenges.

The key principles include promoting technical excellence, fostering networking opportunities, and facilitating the commercialization of groundbreaking technologies.

  • Accessibility First: Every event, session, and material should be accessible to people with different abilities, knowledge levels, and backgrounds.
  • Community-Driven: TechCon thrives on the active participation and feedback of its community—decisions are made with attendees’ growth and benefit in mind.
  • Open Knowledge Sharing: Encourage open-source culture, transparent knowledge exchange, and mentorship opportunities.
  • Sustainability: Commitment to environmentally conscious event planning and promotion of tech solutions that contribute to sustainability goals.
  • Ethical Technology: Champion the responsible use of technology, with discussions around data privacy, AI ethics, and the social implications of technological advancement.
